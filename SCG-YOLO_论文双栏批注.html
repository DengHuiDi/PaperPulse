论文原文 — 摘要

Abstract—With the ongoing progress in remote sensing object detection, small-object detection in complex environments still faces difficulties. The representation of small-object features is often limited, and the localization accuracy can be insufficient, which continues to constrain further improvements in overall performance. To address these challenges, we introduce SCG-YOLO, an improved framework based on YOLO11. First, a multidirectional shift channel upsampling (MSCU) module applies a shift channel mix mechanism. By performing directional cyclic shifts and reorganizing features across grouped channels, MSCU recovers high-frequency spatial details lost in downsampling. Second, the C3K2-structured context-guided block (C3K2-CGB) is built by replacing the bottleneck in the backbone's C3K2 block with a context-guided block (CGB). Using a dual-branch design to capture local details and broader context separately, followed by global refinement, CGB enhances small-object representation in complex backgrounds. Finally, the swish-gated linear unit (SwiGLU)-based localization quality module is integrated into the detection head, where its more stable gradient behavior and more effective feature gating help improve the reliability of bounding box quality assessment for small objects, thereby supporting more accurate localization. On the NWPU-10 and SIMD datasets, SCG-YOLO achieves mAP@0.5 of 85.5% and 82.3%, representing gains of 3.2 and 1.6 percentage points over the baseline.

论文原文 — I. 引言
研究背景与应用场景

SMALL-OBJECT detection in remote sensing imagery is a key area in computer vision, with applications in disaster monitoring [1], military reconnaissance, and environmental assessment. The widespread deployment of high-resolution satellites and UAVs has generated vast amounts of data, making manual interpretation increasingly impractical. Remote sensing images present unique challenges, including large variations in object scales, complex backgrounds, a high proportion of small objects, and arbitrary orientations, which complicate conventional detection methods.

技术发展与相关工作

Object detection has progressed from handcrafted feature-based methods to deep learning frameworks. R-CNN [2] introduced CNNs for detection, establishing the two-stage paradigm. Faster R-CNN [3] further incorporated a region proposal network (RPN) for end-to-end training. Single-stage detectors such as YOLO [4] and SSD [5] improved efficiency by directly predicting object locations and classes. More recently, transformer-based methods like DETR [6] enhanced detection performance. Among these, the YOLO series remains popular for its efficiency, with YOLOv11 employing adaptive feature enhancement and dynamic IoU optimization to improve precision in complex scenarios.

Several YOLO-based variants have focused on improving feature representation and robustness for small-object detection. For example, Fang and Wang [7] proposed a cross-modal attention fusion module. Yao et al. [8] introduced SRM-YOLO. Li et al. [9] developed SOD-YOLO with an enhanced YOLOv8 backbone.

现存问题与本文贡献

Despite these advances, detecting small objects remains challenging due to weak feature representation and inaccurate localization in complex backgrounds. To address these issues, we propose a high-performance small-object detection model based on an improved YOLO11. The main contributions are as follows.

1) SCG-YOLO, an enhanced YOLO11-based framework, incorporates three key modules: the multidirectional shift channel upsampling (MSCU) for detail recovery, the C3K2-structured context-guided block (C3K2-CGB) for backbone feature enhancement, and the swish-gated linear unit (SwiGLU)-based geometric quality assessor (SGQA) integrated into the detection head to improve localization.

2) MSCU, which uses shift channel mixing and channel shuffling to restore high-frequency details and enhance small-object feature representation and localization.

3) C3K2-CGB, designed to enhance feature representation and robustness in complex backgrounds through local-dilated contextual processing.

4) SGQA, employing a dual-branch gated structure to learn bounding box corner distributions, improving sensitivity to subtle localization differences and consistency between classification confidence and box accuracy.

论文原文 — II. 方法论

The SCG-YOLO network architecture is shown in Fig. 1. To reduce detail loss during upsampling, the MSCU module performs directional spatial shifts across channels, enabling cross-channel interaction and enhancing high-frequency detail recovery with minimal extra parameters. For improved detection of small and weak targets in complex backgrounds, the C3K2-CGB module is formed by replacing the C3K2 bottleneck. The module integrates local context with higher level semantics to improve feature representation. To address inconsistencies between bounding box localization and classification confidence, the detection head employs an SGQA. The gated mechanism enhances bounding box quality perception and calibrates prediction scores.

论文原文 — A. MSCU模块
多方向移位通道上采样

In small-object detection from remote sensing imagery, standard nearest neighbor upsampling is efficient but performs spatial interpolation, which limits its ability to recover high-frequency details lost during deep downsampling and can degrade localization accuracy. To address this, the MSCU module is introduced. Built upon the efficient upsampling convolutional block [10], MSCU incorporates a shift-based channel mixing mechanism to enhance spatial representation with minimal computational cost. Unlike conventional channel shuffle, which merely reorders channels, the proposed shift operation enables spatial interaction across channel groups through directional cyclic shifts.

The forward process of MSCU is formulated as:

MSCU(X) = Dpw ⊙ Sshift ⊙ CS ⊙ Ddw ⊙ Up(X) ... (1)

where Up(·) denotes 2× nearest neighbor upsampling, Ddw is a 3 × 3 depthwise convolution for enhancing local details, and CS(·) performs channel shuffle to strengthen cross-group communication. The operator Sshift(·) redistributes spatial cues by shifting channel groups in different directions, while Dpw generates adaptive fusion weights for channel recalibration.

As shown in Fig. 2(b), features are evenly divided into four groups, each shifted horizontally or vertically before concatenation. This directional redistribution allows channel subsets to perceive complementary spatial regions, improving fine-grained feature representation for small-object detection.

论文原文 — B. C3K2-CGB模块
C3K2结构化的上下文引导块

To improve feature extraction in complex backgrounds, we replace the C3K2 bottleneck in YOLO11 with the CGB [11]. The standard C3K2 uses single-scale convolutions, limiting its ability to capture both local and global context for small objects in cluttered scenes.

CGB, as illustrated in Fig. 3, splits feature extraction into two parallel branches: Floc uses 3 × 3 depthwise separable convolutions for fine-grained textures, while Fscur employs dilated convolutions for broader semantic context. The fused features are processed as:

joi_feat = σ(BP(Floc(X'), Fd_sur(X'))) ... (2)

Y = Fglo(joi_feat) + X ... (3)

where X' = F1×1(X) is the reduced feature, and Fglo performs global refinement. CGB enhances small-object features while suppressing background interference through multiscale fusion and attention mechanisms.

论文原文 — C. SGQA模块
基于SwiGLU的几何质量评估器

Accurate assessment of bounding box quality is a critical challenge in small-object detection for remote sensing images. Traditional location quality estimators (LQEs) [12] typically use MLP with ReLU activations to predict bounding box scores. However, ReLU's hard thresholding can cause gradient vanishing, limiting the ability to capture subtle features of small objects.

To address this, SwiGLU is introduced, originally from large language models, to enhance feature learning in LQE. Unlike standard MLP:

h(l) = ReLU(W(l)h(l-1) + b(l)) ... (4)

SwiGLU employs a gated dual-branch structure:

h(l) = SiLU(Wgate·h(l-1)) ⊙ (Wup·h(l-1)) ... (5)

where SiLU(x) = x · σ(x).

In the improved LQE, bounding box regression outputs are first softmax-normalized. For each corner, the top-k probabilities and their mean are extracted as statistical features, which are then processed by a multilayer SwiGLU network to generate a quality adjustment score. The final score is obtained by fusing this quality score with the original classification score:

Sfinal = Scls + Q ... (6)

SwiGLU provides three main benefits: 1) smooth activation, which stabilizes gradients and mitigates vanishing; 2) adaptive feature gating, which modulates feature propagation based on input, enhancing robustness; and 3) enhanced nonlinearity, introducing richer feature interactions for finer perception of bounding box quality differences.

论文原文 — III. 实验
A. 实验设置

The training settings are summarized in Table I. This study employs the public NWPU-10 and SIMD remote sensing datasets for model training. The NWPU-10 dataset is split into training, validation, and test sets with a 6:2:2 ratio, while the SIMD dataset is divided into training and validation sets using an 8:2 split. All experiments are carried out on a cloud server running Ubuntu 22.04 and equipped with a single NVIDIA RTX 3080Ti GPU (12 GB).

B. 对比实验

As shown in Table III, on NWPU-10, SCG-YOLO attains 85.5% mAP@0.5, outperforming YOLOv12n (+2.1%) and YOLOv13n (+2.5%) at similar complexity, while using fewer resources than higher accuracy models like CRKD-YOLO [13] (−13.3 GFLOPs and −3.0 M parameters) and maintaining higher accuracy than HAF-YOLO [14] (+0.5% at −1.8 M parameters and −5.4 GFLOPs).

Similarly, on SIMD, in Table IV, SCG-YOLO reaches 82.3% mAP@0.5, exceeding YOLOv12n (+2.6%) and YOLOv13n (+1.7%). Compared to AAD-YOLO [18], it achieves comparable accuracy (−0.4%) with lower resource usage (−1.0 M parameters, −1.7 GFLOPs).

C. 消融实验

Individual Module Analysis: On NWPU VHR-10, SGQA demonstrates the highest efficiency (11.0 pp/GFLOP) with minimal speed cost (−5.8% FPS), adding only 0.1 GFLOPs for a 1.1-pp gain. MSCU shows moderate efficiency (2.0 pp/GFLOP) with similar speed impact (−5.2%). CGB reduces GFLOPs by 0.4 while achieving a 1.0-pp gain but incurs a 24.7% FPS drop.

Combined Configurations: The MSCU+SGQA combination achieves practical efficiency (4.6 pp/GFLOP on NWPU and 2.8 pp/GFLOP on SIMD) while maintaining throughput above 113 FPS. The full model reaches peak accuracy (85.5% and 82.3% mAP@0.5) but with substantial speed trade-offs (86.1 and 87.5 FPS, approximately −35% across datasets).

Deployment Recommendations: These results suggest task-specific module selection: SGQA alone for latency-critical applications (offering 9–11 pp/GFLOP with <6% speed cost), MSCU+SGQA for balanced scenarios requiring >110 FPS with 2+ pp gains, and the full configuration where accuracy justifies ~35% slower inference.

论文原文 — IV. 结论

This letter introduces SCG-YOLO, an enhanced framework based on YOLOv11n for small-object detection in remote sensing images. The MSCU module restores high-frequency spatial details to improve localization accuracy. In the backbone, the C3K2-CGB employs a dual-branch structure to capture both local details and contextual information, refined through global attention for better feature representation in complex backgrounds. An SGQA is also integrated into the detection head, utilizing a dual-branch design to enhance bounding box evaluation.

Experiments on the NWPU-10 and SIMD datasets demonstrate improved performance across multiple metrics, validating the effectiveness of each module. Analysis indicates that contextual modeling can be further optimized in dense scenes, while inference efficiency remains to be enhanced. Future work will focus on lightweight architecture design and scene-adaptive improvements to strengthen generalization and deployment in diverse remote sensing scenarios.
